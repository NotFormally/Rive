*Date: February 2026*

## 1. Vector A: LinkedIn Outbound (Direct to Decision Maker)

LinkedIn is the ultimate platform for B2B SaaS. We don't need to be "stealthy" here; we just need to be professional and provide immense value upfront.

### Targeting
- **Titles:** Restaurant Owner, General Manager, F&B Director, Concept Developer.
- **Filters:** Independent restaurant groups (2-10 locations) in your target region.

### The 3-Step Sequence

**Message 1: The Connection Request (No pitch, purely networking)**
> "Hi [Name], I'm connecting with fellow hospitality operators in [City/Region]. I've been following [Restaurant Name] and love what you're doing. I operate in the F&B tech space and am trying to connect with leaders navigating the current food cost inflation. Would love to add you to my network."

**Message 2: The Value Drop (Sent 2 days after acceptance)**
> "Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I noticed a lot of operators are currently struggling with 'invisible inflation' from suppliers slowly bumping prices on high-volume items. 
> 
> My team recently built a completely free framework (and automated tool) that maps theoretical vs. actual pour/food cost variance to stop the bleeding without raising menu prices. I thought it might be relevant for [Restaurant Name]. Are you currently tracking variance manually or using a specific POS integration?"

**Message 3: The Follow-up (Sent 4 days later if no reply)**
> "Hey [Name], I know how busy service gets. Just floating this to the top of your inbox. If you have 5 minutes this week, I’d love to show you how the variance tracker works—no strings attached, just looking for feedback from experienced operators like yourself. Cheers."

## 2. Vector B: Facebook Groups (The "Uncensored" Community)

Unlike Reddit, Facebook groups for restaurant owners are less anonymous and often more tolerant of founders sharing tools—**if** it helps the community.

### Target Groups
- **Restaurant Owners Uncensored** (Very active, very raw).
- **Restaurant Owners, Managers & Chefs**
- Local regional FB groups (e.g., "NYC Restaurant Owners").

### Rules of Engagement
1. **Never post a link in the original post.** Just like the successful Reddit stealth post, end with "DM me" or "Comment below and I'll send it."
2. **Use visuals.** A screenshot of a messy Excel sheet vs. a sleek Rive dashboard works wonders on Facebook.

### Group Post Template (The "Excel Nightmare")
> **Attached Image:** A blurry photo of a chaotic kitchen prep list or a massive, confusing Excel spreadsheet.
>
> "Is anyone else still running their multi-million dollar business on a mix of WhatsApp groups and broken Excel sheets? 🤯
>
> We were losing our minds trying to calculate true food cost variance because our POS data never matched the supplier invoices. We finally spent the last few months building a dedicated "operating system" just to sync our POS, track live variance, and digitize our shift logbooks. 
>
> It completely changed how our managers run the floor. If anyone is sick of fighting Excel every Sunday morning and wants to see the tool we built, drop a comment below and I'll DM you the link to check it out."
